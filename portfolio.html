Necropolitics and Mining in the Philippines: Exploring the Social System Dynamics in Local Communities

Behavioral Science, Social Systems Theory

This literature review examines the intricate dynamics between necropolitics, social systems theory, and the impact on mining-affected communities in the Philippines. This paper examines the prevalence of accumulation by dispossession, in which capitalist progress is pushed through privatization of community lands, with long-term ramifications for small-scale gold miners in the area. The influence of the capitalist world system encourages capital interests while reinforcing community marginalization and inequality within the mining sector. Hazardous surroundings and social death placed on disadvantaged groups reveal necropolitical behaviors. Mining activities in the Philippines have serious environmental and health repercussions, as well as abuses of human rights and challenges to environmental justice. Nonetheless, mining communities show resiliency via advocacy and empowerment. Addressing environmental and health concerns, supporting human rights, empowering communities, and improving sustainable mining methods are all policy implications. By analyzing the social system dynamics within local communities affected by mining, this paper seeks to shed light on the socio-political implications and consequences of resource extraction in the country.

Exploring Trends in Wholesale Goods Prices Per Region as Economic Indicators

Data Science, Economic Trends

The paper aims to investigate the dynamics of wholesale goods pricing over time, taking into account the effects of inflation, geographical variances, and economic indicators. The paper includes a comprehensive analysis that includes adjusting prices for inflation, assessing trends in average prices, identifying places with the most/least costly prices, and investigating relationships with GRDP and GRDP per capita. The primary objectives of this paper are to: 1. Analyze wholesale goods prices over time, considering inflation, regional variations, and economic indicators. 2. Investigate trends in average prices and identify regions with the most/least expensive prices. 3. Assess the consistency of price changes over time and their reflection in GRDP or GRDP per capita data. 4. Explore the pricing dynamics of different agricultural commodities.

The Challenges of Waste Segregation and No Plastic Policy in De La Salle University - Manila

Environmental Anthropology, Waste Segregation

This study aims to examine the challenges faced in the implementation of waste segregation and the no plastic policy in De La Salle University â€“ Manila, and to identify potential measures to improve the effectiveness of these initiatives in the institution. The theoretical framework used in this study is based on Fishbein and Ajzen's Theory of Reasoned Action (TRA), which suggests that an individual's behavior is determined by their intention to perform that behavior, which is influenced by their attitudes, subjective norms, and perceived behavioral control. The study utilized both quantitative and qualitative research methods, including surveys and interviews, to gather data from the university community, including students, faculty, and staff. The findings suggest that while the implementation of waste segregation and the no plastic policy has been successful in raising awareness about waste management, there are still challenges in adhering to these policies due to the lack of infrastructure and support. Furthermore, the study identified potential measures to improve the effectiveness of these initiatives, including the need for better communication and education, the establishment of a waste management system, and the promotion of sustainable alternatives. Overall, this study highlights the importance of waste management and the need for proactive measures to address the challenges of plastic waste and land-based environmental debris.

Nurturing a Collective Dream: Vision, National Identity, anf the Socioeconomic Landscape of Filipino Workers

Behavioral Science, Labor Migration

This paper delves into the complex web of socioeconomic elements that support the long-standing pattern of Filipino migration. The article also seeks to uncover the complex characteristics of the Filipino diaspora and its consequences for the nation's development trajectory using a social science lens by deconstructing historical injustices, economic inequality, and their impact on national identity. By doing this, this paper hopes to pave the way for informed discussions and policies that correspond with the collective aspiration of a brighter, more empowered future for Filipino workers.
